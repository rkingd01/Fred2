Targeting non-Russians

Stalin's secret police (Cheka or NKVD) successfully 'removed' the prosperous peasant class via a campaign of terror which spread across the country including the Baltics and Ukraine. The image below purports to show infants of the 'removed'. Orphaned by Cheka.



alphahistory.com


Of the families being dispossessed and deported in the Ingria region non-Russian families were particularly affected. Anni Reuter provides a personalised account of the the Jääskeläinen family.

An essay by Ian Matley's on 'The Dispersal of the Ingrian Finns' is freely available complete with references to sources here.

Under the heading of 'Population transfer in the Soviet Union' Wikipedia presents verified data tabularised to show the different ethnic groups transported to the unpopulated and under-populated areas of the Soviet Union. During the mid 1930s 420,000 Finnic peoples were deported as punishment for the 'crime' of belonging to the affluent class.
